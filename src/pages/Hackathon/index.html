Hackathon Guidelines and Process

All participants must adhere to the following guidelines

  • Rule 1

    Use Online Resources

    You are allowed to use online resources like tutorials and libraries, but make sure to give credit to the sources you use.

  • Rule 2

    Teamwork

    Teams are mandatory for better documentation and clear explanations, ensuring a well-rounded and organized project.

  • Rule 3

    48-Hour Time Frame

    You have exactly 48 hours to work on your project at home or any preferred location. After the 48 hours, you will have a specific window to submit your project.

  • Rule 4

    Original Work

    Your project must be built from scratch during the hackathon. If any prior work or code is used, it must be clearly stated.

  • Rule 5

    Project Demo

    Include a brief video or written description explaining how your project works when submitting. That's why teamwork is mandatory .

  • Rule 6

    Evaluation Criteria

    Projects will be judged based on innovation, complexity, user experience, and completeness.

  • Rule 7

    Stay Professional

    Maintain a positive and respectful attitude throughout the event.

  • Rule 8

    Disqualification Rule

    If your project is found to be copied or purchased from the internet, or if any team member absent on the submission day, the entire team will be disqualified, no matter how super or great your project is.

The hackathon was specifically for the IT Department's 2nd and 3rd-year students, and we are proud to say that we pioneered this exciting initiative.
